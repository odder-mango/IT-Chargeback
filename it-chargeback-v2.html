Guiding Principles
Promote Responsible Consumption
Encourage thoughtful, justified use of IT services and resources across all departments.
Deter Non-Standard Usage
Reduce duplicate and siloed systems that add complexity and maintenance costs.
Not for Profit
Cost recovery only — the programme covers maintenance costs, not surplus generation.
Expected Benefits
Enable the University to Do More with Less
Rationalise IT spend to free budget for innovation and strategic investment.
Every Dollar Delivers Clear Value
Align IT investment directly to academic, research and administrative outcomes.
Reduce Risks from Siloed Systems
Minimise technical debt, security vulnerabilities and integration complexity.
System Tiers
System Classification Framework
All IT systems fall into one of three tiers. Phase 1 focuses on Business Tactical Systems ↓
View Classification Guide →
Tier 1
Enterprise IT Platforms
Foundational systems providing shared capabilities across the university — data warehousing, AI infrastructure, and system integration.
Data Warehouse AI Platforms Integration
Tier 2
Business Strategic Systems
Systems that support university-wide objectives and are integral to the University's core academic, research and administrative functions.
University-wide Core Functions
Tier 3 — Phase 1 Scope
Business Tactical Systems
Unit-specific systems that serve internal or localised needs. Maintenance costs for these systems are subject to chargeback recovery.
Unit-specific Localised Needs
